Bid Cost Sheet (Private)

I like “TrueCost™ Profit Sheet” because it sounds premium and it’s clear this is internal/private.

✅ Store it inside each bid as:
Bid → “TrueCost™ Profit Sheet (Private)”

1) Step-by-step: How the Contractor Uses It (Super Clear)
Step 1 — Open a bid

Contractor selects an opportunity in the procurement dashboard.

Step 2 — Click “TrueCost™ Profit Sheet (Private)”

This opens a private job costing screen.

Step 3 — Enter Bid Amount & Terms (top section)

Bid amount (what they are submitting)

Payment terms (Net 30/45/60 if known)

Mobilization/demob (if included)

Retainage % (if any)

Tax (if applicable)

Step 4 — Build the Job Plan (labor + equipment + materials)

Contractor adds line items in 3 tabs:

Labor

Equipment

Materials/Subs
(+ optional: Overhead/Insurance/Fuel)

Step 5 — Enter production assumptions

Job duration (days)

Work hours/day

Crew count

Expected overtime rules (if any)

Step 6 — Profit output appears instantly

The app shows:

Total cost to perform the job

Gross profit ($)

Gross margin (%)

Net profit ($) after overhead/insurance

Break-even bid price

Profit per day

Profit per crew hour

Cash needed up front

Step 7 — Save it under the bid

It’s saved under that bid’s record and is private to the contractor.

Each labor row:

Role (Foreman, Operator, Climber, Groundman, etc.)

Qty (number of workers)

Pay type: hourly or daily

Base rate ($/hr)

Burden % (payroll taxes, WC, benefits) OR fixed burden $/hr

Hours regular

Hours overtime

OT multiplier (1.5 default)

Per diem (if any)

Lodging (if charged per person/day)

Notes

✅ App auto-calculates:
Labor Cost = (Reg hours × rate + OT hours × rate × multiplier) × qty + per diem + lodging + burden

Burden (important)

Contractors forget this. Include a default toggle:

Burden Mode

% of wage (recommended)

$/hr add-on

Small contractor: 18–28% typical (varies widely)

3) Profit Math Logic (What the App Should Compute)
Core outputs

Let:

BidRevenue = Bid amount
DirectLabor = sum labor costs
EquipmentCost = sum equipment costs
MaterialsCost = sum materials/sub costs
JobOverhead = job-level overhead & insurance allocation
Contingency = optional % × (DirectLabor + EquipmentCost + MaterialsCost)
PaymentFees = optional (card fees, factoring, etc.)

Total Cost

TotalJobCost = DirectLabor + EquipmentCost + MaterialsCost + JobOverhead + Contingency + PaymentFees

Profit & Margin

GrossProfit = BidRevenue - (DirectLabor + EquipmentCost + MaterialsCost)
NetProfit = BidRevenue - TotalJobCost
NetMargin% = NetProfit / BidRevenue

Break-even bid

BreakEvenBid = TotalJobCost / (1 - desiredMargin%) (optional tool)

Cash needed upfront (killer feature)

Contractors care about cash flow. Add:

% materials due upfront

payroll cycle weekly

fuel weekly

rental deposits

Compute:
UpfrontCashNeeded = material deposits + first payroll + fuel + rentals + lodging

That’s extremely valuable in storm work.
