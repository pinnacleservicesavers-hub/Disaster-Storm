Here are the official links you can use to register or find contractor/vendor procurement opportunities with electric utilities, co-ops, and municipal electric systems in Delaware — so you can find work (bids/contracts, supplier registration, vendor lists, or procurement contacts):

⚡ 1. Statewide Procurement Portal (All Sectors)

🔗 Delaware’s Procurement Portal (Vendor Registration & Bidding)
https://mymarketplace.delaware.gov/

✔ Register as a vendor for state, local & municipal contracts
✔ View current bid solicitations and awarded contracts
✔ Receive bid notifications
✔ Find vendor resources & contact lists

👉 Specifically for suppliers/contractors:
🔗 Vendor Resources & Supplier Tools
https://mymarketplace.delaware.gov/vendors/index.shtml

✔ eSupplier portal
✔ Bid notification registration
✔ Procurement contact information

⚡ 2. Delaware Electric Cooperative (Co-Op)

📌 This is the main electric cooperative serving Kent & Sussex Counties.

🔗 Delaware Electric Cooperative – Main Website
https://www.delaware.coop/

📌 Important Supplier / Third Party Information
https://www.delaware.coop/important-information-suppliers

✔ Details on becoming a certified supplier
✔ Requirements and contact info for energy suppliers

📌 Supplier Agreements & Credit Application (PDF downloads)
https://www.delaware.coop/about/deregulation/credit-application

✔ Download forms needed to apply as a supplier/vendor

Note: This page mainly covers third party energy supplier certification — for contracting work you may still need to contact DEC directly via their contact form or procurement office.

⚡ 3. Delaware Municipal Electric Corporation (DEMEC)

DEMEC is the joint action agency for municipal electric utilities in DE (Dover, Newark, Milford, Smyrna, Clayton, Lewes, New Castle, Middletown, Seaford).

🔗 DEMEC Official Website
https://www.demecinc.net/

👉 DEMEC itself may not have a public vendor registration page, but member utilities most often use DEMEC for power supply and each member utility has procurement/vendor processes.

⚡ 4. Municipal Electric Utilities (Member Cities)

These cities run their own utilities and most have procurement/bids pages:

City of Newark Electric & Procurement

🔗 Newark Vendor / Bids & Opportunities
https://newarkde.gov/Bids.aspx

✔ RFPs/ITBs for electrical, construction & services
✔ Vendor contact info for procurement

📌 City of Newark Procurement Office Info (Vendor Contacts)
https://newarkde.gov/105/Purchasing

✔ Purchasing department contacts for vendor registration and bid questions

⚡ 5. Additional Municipal Utilities (No direct vendor pages found but worth contacting)

These utilities each manage electric services and sometimes list bid/vendor contacts on their municipal sites:

✔ City of Dover Electric Department – Search “Dover vendor opportunities” on https://www.cityofdover.com

✔ City of Milford Electric Utility – Search “vendor / purchasing” on https://www.cityofmilford.com

✔ City of Seaford Electric Utility – Search “bids / purchasing” on https://www.seafordde.com

✔ Lewes Board of Public Works (Electric services) – https://www.lewesbpw.com

✔ City of New Castle MSC (electric portion) – https://newcastlemsc.com

✔ Town of Smyrna Electric – https://www.smyrnade.com

Tip: Look under each city’s “Doing Business With Us,” “Bids & RFPs,” “Purchasing,” or “Procurement” sections.

🏛️ Extra Resources for Utilities & Energy Suppliers
Delaware Public Service Commission (Certified Electric Supplier List)

https://depsc.delaware.gov/customer-electric-choice/

✔ Lists certified electric suppliers allowed to serve in DE — helpful for understanding the energy supplier side of procurement.
