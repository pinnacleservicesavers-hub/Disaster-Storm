If you’re asking why Strategic Land Management (or any contractor) should register with USACE districts, here is the straight, strategic answer:

Why You Must Register With USACE Districts
1️⃣ USACE Does NOT Automatically Know You Exist

Even if:

Your SAM is active

You have a CAGE code

You’re veteran-owned

You’re storm-ready

That does not mean a USACE District will ever see your company.

Each district operates like its own contracting office.
If you don’t market directly to them, you are invisible.

2️⃣ Storm Work Is Activated at the District Level

Emergency debris, vegetation clearing, and emergency power contracts are usually managed by specific districts such as:

USACE Mobile District

USACE Savannah District

USACE Jacksonville District

USACE New Orleans District

When a hurricane hits:

👉 Those districts activate contractors already in their system
👉 They call companies they already know
👉 They refer primes to subcontractors they’ve seen before

If you’re not registered or introduced, you don’t get called.

3️⃣ Small Business Specialists Can Advocate for You

Every district has a Small Business Office.

Their job is to:

Increase veteran-owned participation

Increase small business participation

Help primes find qualified subcontractors

If you register and introduce yourself:

You get added to vendor lists

You get invited to industry days

You may get included in subcontract referrals

That is how relationships start.

4️⃣ Many Contracts Are IDIQ / ACI / MATOC

These are:

Indefinite Delivery Indefinite Quantity (IDIQ)

Advance Contracting Initiative (ACI)

Multiple Award Task Order Contracts (MATOC)

They are awarded BEFORE storms.

When the storm hits:

They activate existing contract holders

No new vendors get added

If you miss the award window, your only way in is as a subcontractor — and primes will search district vendor lists.

5️⃣ It Positions You as “Ready”

When you contact a district and say:

“We are SAM active, veteran-owned, arborist-certified, fully insured, and can mobilize within 24 hours.”

You are positioning yourself as:

Low risk

Storm ready

Pre-qualified

Federal aware

That builds credibility before you ever bid.

6️⃣ It Increases Subcontracting Opportunities

Large primes like:

AshBritt

CrowderGulf

DRC Emergency Services

Ceres Environmental Services

Often ask districts:

“Do you know any qualified local small businesses?”

If your name is on file, you get recommended.

In Simple Terms

Registering with districts:

✔ Makes you visible
✔ Makes you eligible
✔ Builds relationships
✔ Positions you before disasters
✔ Opens subcontracting doors
✔ Gets you on radar for future MATOC awards

Without it, you are waiting and hoping.

With it, you are positioned.
