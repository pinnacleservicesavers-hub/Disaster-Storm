Maxar Technologies — What It Is

Maxar Technologies is (or was) a major U.S. space and satellite technology company focused on Earth observation, geospatial intelligence, and satellite services.

It provided some of the highest-resolution commercial satellite imagery available — often used for mapping, disaster monitoring, climate and environmental tracking, infrastructure planning, and intelligence.

Maxar’s imagery has been widely used in news and research, such as tracking natural disasters or monitoring events like conflicts and infrastructure changes.

In 2025 Maxar reorganized its companies (with its imaging business becoming Vantor), but its satellite imagery legacy continues through platforms and data APIs.

In practice, Maxar imagery can show detailed before-and-after pictures of storms, hurricanes, flooding, and land changes — which is why organizations tracking major weather events often use their satellite data to understand impacts on the ground.

📱 SpyMeSat — Satellite Imagery & Tracking App

SpyMeSat isn’t a satellite itself — it’s a mobile/web application that gives users access to commercial satellite imagery and tracking data.

Here’s what SpyMeSat does:

📍 Satellite Overflight Tracking: It shows when imaging satellites (from providers like Maxar, Planet, etc.) are passing overhead and could capture imagery — essentially telling you when satellites could see a location.

🛰️ Archive Imagery Marketplace: Users can browse and purchase existing satellite images of any location.

🖼️ Tasking New Images: You can request that a satellite take a new image of a specific place (a “tasking request”) — even multiple hours ahead.

So in the storm context, people might use SpyMeSat to quickly get recent high-resolution satellite images of an area affected by a storm, or to see when a satellite might next pass overhead.

🌀 How These Relate to Storm Tracking / Monitoring

Maxar’s satellite imagery is high-resolution and frequently used by analysts, insurers, emergency responders, and media to see damage after a storm or to observe changes over time.

SpyMeSat makes satellite imagery (potentially including Maxar’s images) more accessible to the public or specialists without needing large contracts — letting users see very recent images and even task new ones around storms or disasters.
