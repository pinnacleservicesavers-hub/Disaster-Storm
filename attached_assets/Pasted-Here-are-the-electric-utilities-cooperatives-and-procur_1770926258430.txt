Here are the electric utilities, cooperatives, and procurement/vendor registration resources in Delaware — including the links you can use to get work with them as a contractor/vendor and where to find the procurement or supplier pages.

⚡ 1. Delaware Electric Cooperative

🔗 Official Website: https://www.delaware.coop/

This is the main rural electric cooperative (member-owned utility) in Delaware, serving Kent and Sussex Counties.

📌 Supplier / Certified Supplier Info — The Co-op has a process to become a certified supplier, which includes filling out a credit application, supplier agreement, and returning them after any required licensing/certification with the Delaware Public Service Commission.

📌 What to Do Next for Contractors:

Contact the energy supply or procurement contact at DEC (email listed on supplier doc pages).

Complete any required credit/supplier agreement forms and return as directed.

You may need certification through the state PSC and PJM Interconnection first for power supply services.

⚡ 2. Delaware Municipal Electric Corporation (DEMEC)

🔗 Website: http://www.demecinc.net/

DEMEC is a joint action agency and wholesale electric utility representing nine municipal electric departments in Delaware (e.g., Dover, Newark, Milford).

📌 Why Register: DEMEC may issue projects or requirements for vendors/interconnect work, and its municipal members often have procurement/vendor opportunities.

👉 After registering with DEMEC, you can connect with individual member utilities for contracting.

⚡ 3. Delmarva Power

🔗 Company Site: https://www.delmarva.com/

Delmarva Power is the largest electric and natural gas utility in Delaware (part of Exelon), serving much of the state.

📌 Supplier/Contractor Registration:
Delmarva Power does not publish a public vendor registration link on the main page, but its procurement and supplier information for contractors is typically managed through Exelon’s supplier portal:

Search for “Exelon Supplier Registration” on the Exelon corporate site or contact procurement directly.

🏛️ Delaware State Procurement Portal (All Sectors)

If you want to work with the state government (including municipal or utility-related contracts, even outside utilities):

🔗 Vendor registration / procurement portal: https://mymarketplace.delaware.gov/vendors/vendor-registration.shtml

Here you can:
✔ Register your business
✔ Receive email alerts for bids (RFPs, ITBs, RFIs)
✔ Access bid directory & contract listings
✔ See opportunities with state agencies, municipalities, and school districts

This is valuable because utilities and public entities sometimes post work here as well.

🧠 Why Register With These (Contractor Perspective)

📌 Utilities and cooperatives require vendor registration before you can:
• Be considered for contracts and solicitations
• Be paid for work in their system
• Receive notice of upcoming opportunities

📌 Co-ops like DEC require supplier agreements and documentation before work can begin — often including credit applications and compliance with regulatory certifications.

📌 Municipal utilities and DEMEC members may have unique registration processes — many accept contractor lists for tree/vegetation work, storm response, or system maintenance.
