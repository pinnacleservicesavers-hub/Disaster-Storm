What “Geo-Capture” Means
Geo-capture (or geo-fencing) means digitally drawing a fence on a map so you can tag every smartphone or device that enters or lives inside that area.
Once you tag them, you can:
	1	Show ads on their phones, social apps, or YouTube later.
	2	Build an audience list for follow-up ads or text/email campaigns.
	3	Trigger messages or calls-to-action the moment a storm passes.
It’s like placing a digital billboard in someone’s pocket—but only in the exact neighborhood that just got hit.

⚙️ How Geo-Capture Works Step-by-Step
1. Define the Target Area
When a storm like Jerry forms, use a storm-impact map (from RadarOmega, Windy, or Stormers.io) to see:
	•	Counties with gusts 55+ mph
	•	ZIPs in the projected cone
	•	or a 3-mile buffer around the forecasted damage path
You can literally outline that polygon in ad software.
📍 Example: Draw a 25-mile circle around Panama City → that becomes your “geo-fence.”

3. Capture the Devices
As soon as a phone’s GPS or IP ping enters your geo-fenced area, the ad network logs it (privacy-safe).
That device is now part of your “custom audience.”
Even after the person leaves the zone (e.g., evacuates or travels), they still see your ads for up to 30 days.

4. Serve Precision Ads
Once the storm passes:
	•	Send ads that say things like:
“Tree on your home? Our emergency crews are already in your neighborhood — Call Shannon 706-840-8949.”

	•	Include a short video of your team working local storm jobs for credibility.
	•	The ad only appears on phones from that geo-fence.
If you’re using GroundTruth or WeatherAds, you can even make the ad auto-activate the moment wind gusts exceed 45 mph in that zone.

5. Layer More Smart Filters
You can combine geo-capture with:
	•	Keyword filters: “tree service,” “roof repair,” “storm cleanup.”
	•	Demographics: homeowners age 30-65 with home-value >$150 k.
	•	Interest layers: insurance, home improvement, gardening.
That ensures you only spend money on homeowners with storm damage — not random passers-by.

6. Follow Up for Weeks
Everyone who entered the geo-fence now forms a retargeting list.
So even a week later, when they finally start searching “tree removal near me,” your ad appears first because the system already “knows” their device came from the damaged zone.

💡 Real-World Example for SLM
Scenario:
Hurricane (or strong tropical storm) makes landfall near Panama City.
What SLM does:
	1	In Meta Ads Manager, create a 25-mile radius around Bay County.
	2	Set your ad copy:
“🚨 Storm Damage? We’re Local, Licensed, and Already Clearing Roads. Free Estimate – Call Shannon at 706-840-8949.”

	3	Activate with WeatherAds trigger: “Show ad when wind gusts >45 mph.”
	4	After landfall, tighten the radius to ZIPs where outages >50%.
	5	Keep retargeting those device IDs for 30 days with videos of your crews at work.
Within 24 hours you’re the first company every local homeowner sees when they open Facebook, Instagram, YouTube, or Google.
