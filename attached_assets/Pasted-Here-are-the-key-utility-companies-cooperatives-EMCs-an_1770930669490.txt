Here are the key utility companies, cooperatives (EMCs), and ways to connect with procurement/vendor registration in Mississippi — including official links and where available supplier pages so you can register your company as a contractor/vendor for work like storm response, vegetation/ROW clearing, emergency restoration, equipment services, and materials supply.

⚡ Investor-Owned Utilities (IOUs)
🔌 Entergy Mississippi – Official Utility Site

Entergy Mississippi is the major electric utility provider in Mississippi, serving ~459,000 customers in 45 counties.

➡️ Procurement / Supplier Info
Entergy’s main corporate site includes supplier pages — search for “suppliers” or “procurement” once there:
https://www.entergy.com/
 (parent site; navigate to supplier details)

Note: Entergy’s specific Mississippi supplier page isn’t public — but their main site links to national supplier info and then you can filter for Entergy Mississippi.

🔌 Mississippi Power – Supplier Registration Info

Mississippi Power is the subsidiary of Southern Company serving the southeastern part of Mississippi.

➡️ Supplier Registration Portal (Smart by GEP)
Access their online portal to self-register as a supplier:
https://www.mississippipower.com/company/supplier-information.html

➤ From that page, you can complete supplier materials/services registrations and submit them to Supply Chain Management for inclusion on their bid lists.

🌲 Electric Cooperatives (EMCs / G&T)
🔌 Cooperative Energy – Mississippi Generation & Transmission Co‑op

Cooperative Energy (formerly South Mississippi Electric Power Association/SMEPA) is the generation & transmission cooperative that powers many local distribution co-ops in Mississippi.

➡️ Main website with contacts (you can start with the Contact/Business page):
https://cooperativeenergy.com/

👉 For contractor/vendor work with the co-op or its members, contact procurement/business development directly through that site.

🔌 Electric Cooperatives of Mississippi – State Association

The statewide trade association representing many distribution cooperatives.

📌 Member Cooperatives Directory & Contacts:
https://www.ecm.coop/

➡ From the “Contact” or “Member Co-ops” sections, you can find individual co-ops in Mississippi and connect with their procurement or operations offices.

🔌 Examples of Local Electric Utilities & Municipal Bid Pages

Some municipal utilities in Mississippi publicly post bids or contracting opportunities — valuable for vendor work beyond EMCs/IOUs:

📍 City of Starkville Utilities – Bid Postings

https://cityofstarkville.org/bids.aspx

➡ Look here for electric division bid notices where you can download specs and submit proposals.

🧠 How to Get Your Company Registered / Working
📌 Investor-Owned Utilities (IOUs)

Entergy Mississippi: Visit the Entergy corporate site and navigate to supplier/vendor info — then contact Entergy Mississippi procurement to get added to their supplier list and receive bid opportunities.

Mississippi Power: Use the Smart by GEP portal to self-register for supplier opportunities.

📌 Cooperatives / EMCs

Contact Cooperative Energy directly for opportunities — many tasks (storm support, equipment, vegetation) go through G&T and member co-ops.

Use the Electric Cooperatives of Mississippi portal to identify local co-ops and contact them about vendor lists, qualifications, and RFPs.

📌 Municipal / Local Utilities

Visit city procurement/bid pages (like Starkville Utilities) for smaller, local RFPs and supplier needs.

🧠 Tips for Contractor Registration

✔ For IOUs, register as a supplier in their official system and submit insurance, W-9, and company details.
✔ For co-ops, reach out to Cooperative Energy and the local co-op members directly for contractor/vendor lists.
✔ For municipal utilities, check their bid/procurement pages regularly for electric service opportunities.
