If you're talking about utility forestry / vegetation management companies, you’re absolutely right — they are often the gatekeepers to large-scale contracts.

Let’s break down why having forestry information, relationships, and positioning is powerful for getting work:

🌲 1. They Control Access to Utility Work

Forestry companies and consulting arborists often manage vegetation contracts for:

Power co-ops

Investor-owned utilities

Municipal electric departments

State & federal right-of-way projects

They don’t always perform the work themselves — they:

Inspect circuits

Identify “linger hangers”

Scope removals

Set specifications

Approve invoices

That makes them the filter between contractors and the utility money.

If you’re connected with them, you’re inside the decision funnel.

⚡ 2. They Influence Who Gets Called During Storms

When storms hit:

Utilities rely on forestry managers for rapid assessments.

Forestry reps recommend contractors.

They often determine priority circuits and urgency.

If they trust your company:

You get first call

You get emergency mobilization opportunities

You get long-term rotation work

In storm response, speed + trust = contracts.

🏗 3. They Understand Compliance (OSHA, ANSI, Utility Specs)

Forestry managers care about:

ANSI A300 standards

ANSI Z133 safety

OSHA compliance

Proper pruning techniques

Clearances around energized lines

If you can speak their language and show:

Certified arborist leadership

Documented safety programs

Equipment capability

Crew training

You immediately elevate above “just a tree company.”

💰 4. They Help Reduce Liability for Utilities

Utilities don’t just want trees cut.

They want:

Reduced outage risk

Reduced fire risk

Reduced public liability

Reduced lawsuit exposure

Forestry companies are hired to protect the utility’s reputation and liability profile.

If you position yourself as:

“We protect infrastructure, reduce risk, and protect your contract reputation.”

You align with what forestry managers are paid to do.

🔑 5. Why They’re Called “Gatekeepers”

Because they:

Recommend contractor approvals

Manage bid scopes

Evaluate performance

Control contractor lists

Influence renewals

If you bypass them, it’s difficult to land consistent utility work.

If you build relationships with them, you gain:

Insider knowledge on upcoming bids

Notice of emergency mobilizations

Insight into problem circuits

Referral power

🎯 Strategic Advantage for SLM

For a company like Strategic Land Management (especially veteran-owned, certified arborist-led, storm-experienced), forestry relationships:

Increase credibility

Shorten sales cycles

Reduce cold-calling

Create repeat revenue streams

Position you for larger prime contracts

🧠 The Big Picture

In utility vegetation management:

Utilities = Funding Source
Forestry Consultants = Gatekeepers
Contractors = Execution

If you want steady revenue and storm deployments, you don’t just chase utilities — you build trust with the forestry managers who advise them.
