I get what you’re trying to say — and you’re not wrong in principle.

More compliant bids + more volume + better data feedback = statistically better chances over time.

BUT… you have to word this very carefully.

You can say it in a performance-logic way.
You cannot say it like a promise.

Here’s how to do it safely.

✅ SAFE VERSION (Marketing + Legal Safe)

You can say:

The more qualified bids you submit, the greater your statistical opportunity to win contracts. Our platform is designed to help contractors submit more compliant, organized, and timely bids — increasing participation and consistency in the bidding process.

That’s safe because:

You’re describing probability.

You’re not guaranteeing results.

You’re tying it to participation volume.

You’re not promising awards.

⚖️ LEGALLY SAFE “Increase Your Odds” LANGUAGE

Add this to your Terms or marketing disclaimer:

Competitive Opportunity Statement

The Platform is designed to help contractors increase bid participation, improve compliance accuracy, and submit proposals more consistently.

In competitive procurement environments, increased participation and improved compliance may enhance a contractor’s statistical probability of award over time.

However, contract awards are determined solely by awarding entities, and no guarantee of success is made or implied.

🛑 DO NOT SAY

Avoid these phrases completely:

“We increase your chances of winning”

“Guaranteed to improve your win rate”

“Proven to win more contracts”

“We win bids for you”

“Our system guarantees more awards”

Those create legal exposure.

📊 If You Want to Be Strong But Safe

Here’s a more powerful but protected version:

Contractors who bid more frequently, stay compliant, and respond quickly to opportunities typically position themselves more competitively in the marketplace. This platform is built to support that consistency and organization — helping you participate more often and more strategically.

That keeps it logical and defensible.

🧠 Why This Matters Legally

If someone pays you $299/month for 6 months and wins nothing, and you said:

“Increase your odds of winning”

They could argue:

“You promised better odds.”

So your language must tie improvement to:

effort

volume

organization

statistical probability

user responsibility

Not to your software directly guaranteeing anything.

🔐 Add This Protective Line to Your Terms

Add this inside your No Guarantee section:

Any reference to “increased odds,” “improved competitiveness,” or similar language refers to general statistical principles of participation volume and organizational efficiency. Such statements are not guarantees of award, profitability, or contract success.

That closes the loophole.
