Here are the major electric utilities and cooperatives in Illinois with official links for procurement, supplier registration, or where to get contractor/vendor work — including investor-owned utilities, rural co-ops, and municipal utilities.

⚡ Investor-Owned Utilities (IOUs) – Supplier/Procurement Links
1. Commonwealth Edison (ComEd) – Official Website

• Largest electric utility in Illinois (serves northern IL including Chicago).
• To work with ComEd as a contractor/vendor, you’ll need to explore their Doing Business / Supplier pages (ComEd manages supplier relationships and has processes for bids and contracts):
👉 Visit ComEd’s official site and look under “Doing Business With Us” for procurement, purchasing, and supplier opportunities: https://www.comed.com/

2. Ameren Illinois – Suppliers / Supplier Portal

• Ameren serves central and southern Illinois.
• Ameren’s supplier page includes information about how to become a supplier, view purchase orders, respond to RFQs, RFPs, and manage compliance.
👉 View Ameren’s procurement/suppliers information here: https://www.ameren.com/partners/suppliers

3. MidAmerican Energy – Supplier Registration

• MidAmerican Energy serves parts of Illinois’s western region and operates a supplier registration portal.
🔗 Supplier Registration & Bid Opportunities:
https://www.midamericanenergy.com/sites/Satellite?c=Page&childpagename=MEC%2FPage%2FStandardPage%2FLayout&cid=1529683759438&pagename=MEC%2FPrimaryWrapper

✔ Register to participate in supplier bid opportunities, receive RFx alerts, and respond to RFPs.

🏙 Municipal / Public Power Utilities

In Illinois, some cities operate electric utilities — and they usually list vendor opportunities on their municipal procurement pages:

City Water, Light & Power (CWLP) – Springfield, IL

🔗 Official Website: http://www.cwlp.com

✔ Municipal utility serving Springfield.

👉 Check their “Business / Supplier / Vendor” or Public Notices / Bids sections for procurement opportunities once on the site.

(Many other municipal utilities in Illinois follow similar patterns — search for “vendor”, “procurement”, or “bids” on the city utility pages to register or find contract opportunities.)

📋 Statewide Energy Procurement (Not Individual Utility but Helpful)
Illinois Power Agency – Competitive Electricity Procurement

🔗 https://ipa.illinois.gov/electricity-procurement.html

This state agency manages large competitive procurements for electricity supply for utilities like Ameren, ComEd, and MidAmerican. This is useful for energy suppliers and project procurement events.

🧠 Additional Notes for Illinois Utilities

Electric suppliers (“Alternative Retail Electric Suppliers”) must be certified via the Illinois Commerce Commission if you intend to supply energy or serve customers in deregulated parts of Illinois.

Most rural cooperatives are part of larger associations or G&T services like Wabash Valley Power Association or Dairyland Power Cooperative, which provide wholesale power to distribution co-ops that serve in Illinois.
