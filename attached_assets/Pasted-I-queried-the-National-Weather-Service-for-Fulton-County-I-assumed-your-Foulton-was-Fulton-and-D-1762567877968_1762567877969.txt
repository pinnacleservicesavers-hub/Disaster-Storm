If a tornado warning is issued (contractor SOP)
1. Safety first — suspend all outdoor operations immediately.
   - Get crews and customers to an interior, windowless room on the lowest floor.
   - Account for everyone and confirm safe sheltering status.
2. Do NOT attempt roofing or tarping during a tornado warning. Wait for NWS “all clear” or cancellation and local EMA confirmation.
3. Prepare for rapid deployment after the all-clear:
   - Pre-staged crews on standby out of the warning path, with phone tree/mass notification ready.
   - Trucks staged at safe distances (outside predicted wind/impact path) but close enough for rapid response.
4. Drone operations:
   - Do NOT fly in an active tornado warning. After the event, conduct aerial damage assessments if FAA airspace authorization allows.
   - Part 107 updates let night operations without waivers if pilot completed updated training and uses anti-collision lighting visible ≥3 statute miles — but controlled airspace still needs authorization.
5. Lightning/wind safety thresholds:
   - Cease external operations if lightning within ~10 miles (or 30 minutes after last thunder).
   - For rooftop operations, plan to have wind sustained under ~25–30 mph for safe work; be conservative (30–35 mph max depending on crew skill and anchorage).
6. Triage priorities after warning expires:
   - Life-safety checks (rescue/EMS coordination).
   - Tarping and board-ups for life/safety and to stop further water entry.
   - Water extraction and moisture mapping.
   - Debris removal to create safe access.
   - Full roof and structural inspections as soon as safe.
