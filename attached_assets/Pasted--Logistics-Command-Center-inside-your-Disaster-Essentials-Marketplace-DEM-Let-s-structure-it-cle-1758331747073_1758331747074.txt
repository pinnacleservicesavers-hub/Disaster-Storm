“Logistics Command Center” inside your Disaster Essentials Marketplace (DEM). Let’s structure it clearly so you can drop it right into your app build:

🚛 Logistics Tab (For Contractors)

This tab would open inside DEM and split by Contractor Type (Tree Services, Roofing, Electrical, Plumbing, General Contractors, etc.).

Each contractor type gets its own AI-powered logistics dashboard that shows them how to plan, travel, and operate during a disaster response.

🔹 Key Features Inside the Logistics Tab
1. Trip Planning & Routing

AI suggests the best routes into the disaster area (live traffic + storm closures + roadblocks).

Fuel stop mapping (with gas station availability, prices, and generator fuel supply).

Highlight restricted access zones (curfews, FEMA staging areas, checkpoints).

Contractor can choose staging hotels within X miles of the work zone.

2. Crew & Equipment Coordination

Contractor enters: number of crew, vehicles, trailers, and equipment.

AI auto-calculates:

How many jobs can be done per day.

Recommended crew rotation (day/night teams).

Best use of equipment (cranes, bucket trucks, skid steers, etc.).

AI assigns crew-to-job dispatches based on distance and job size.

3. Supply Chain Support

AI suggests supplies needed per contractor type:

Tree Services: bar oil, chains, fuel mix, rigging rope, saw parts.

Roofing: tarps, nails, shingles, plywood, sealants.

Electrical: wire, conduit, PPE, generators.

Links directly to local or state-by-state suppliers in DEM.

Alerts when supplies are running low and recommends nearby restock points.

4. AI Logistics Advisor

A chat-style assistant inside the tab where contractors ask:
“How many gallons of fuel will I need for 5 trucks running 10 hours a day?”
“Where should I stage my crews within 20 miles of Fort Myers?”

AI gives detailed, real-time answers.

Predictive planning: “Based on storm track, you’ll need X more chainsaws, Y more gallons of diesel in the next 72 hours.”

5. Shared Resource Coordination (Optional Premium Feature)

Contractors can see other verified contractors in the area (optional “network mode”).

Ability to share resources (like crane rental, extra crews, fuel delivery).

Helps avoid bottlenecks and builds trust with insurance carriers/FEMA.

🔹 Layout Flow

Tab Name: “Logistics”

Step 1: Select contractor type (Tree, Roofing, etc.)

Step 2: Enter trip info (crew, vehicles, destination).

Step 3: AI builds a logistics plan (routes, fuel stops, hotel staging).

Step 4: Live AI advisor answers questions and updates recommendations.

Step 5 (Optional): Network with other contractors in DEM for resource pooling.

🔹 Example (Tree Service Contractor)

2 crews (10 men), 2 bucket trucks, 1 crane, 3 pickups, 2 trailers.

Destination: Fort Myers, FL.

AI Output:

Best route avoiding flooded roads.

Fuel stops with diesel availability.

2 staging hotels with parking for large rigs.

Supply checklist (chainsaw parts, bar oil, PPE).

Job dispatch rotation (Crew A = residential, Crew B = FEMA priority).

👉 This turns DEM from just a “marketplace” into a true operations hub. Contractors not only find supplies — they get battle-ready plans for deployment.
