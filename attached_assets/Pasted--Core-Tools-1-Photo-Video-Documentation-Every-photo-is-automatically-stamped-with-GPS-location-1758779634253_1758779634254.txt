🔑 Core Tools
1. Photo & Video Documentation

Every photo is automatically stamped with GPS location, time, and user.

You don’t have to manually upload or label pictures—saves time.

Great for insurance, liability protection, and proving completion.

2. Project-Based Organization

Each job is its own “project” folder.

All media, notes, tasks, and conversations for that job stay together.

Easy to pull up “Mr. Thomas – 5385 Westwood Dr – Pecan Tree Removal” later if an insurance adjuster calls.

3. Markup & Notes on Photos

Draw arrows, circles, or highlight dangerous limbs or power lines.

Add text notes like “Storm damage – urgent removal needed.”

Adds context to simple photos so anyone looking knows what’s going on.

⚡ Special Features
✅ Project Feed (Live Updates)

Every jobsite update flows into one dashboard.

You can watch crews in the field in real-time without being there.

Ideal for storm response when you’re juggling multiple crews.

✅ Task Lists & Checklists

Example: “Pre-job safety check” → Crew must upload a photo of PPE before starting.

Keeps OSHA compliance tight without paperwork.

Can assign tasks like “Grind stump,” “Haul debris,” and track who did what.

✅ Automatic Reports

AI or prebuilt templates turn photos + notes into:

Daily logs (for internal accountability)

Client updates (so they know progress)

Insurance packets (before/after evidence with descriptions)

Saves hours of admin work.

✅ Client & Subcontractor Sharing

Share a view-only link with homeowners, insurance adjusters, or subcontractors.

They see a timeline or gallery without downloading an app.

Keeps clients calm when they can see progress in real-time.

✅ Document Storage

Upload contracts, permits, work orders, COIs, etc.

Keep everything tied to the same job.

Option to collect e-signatures for authorizations or change orders.

✅ Payments & Invoicing (on certain plans)

Send invoices and request payments from the app.

Handy for on-the-spot collection after emergency work.

✅ Integrations

Works with job management, CRM, and estimating software (e.g. Jobber, Housecall Pro, ServiceTitan, etc.).

Syncs your customer/project info so you’re not retyping.

🌪 Real-World Workflows for Your Business
1. Storm Response / Emergency Tree Removal

Crew arrives, opens the project.

Snap before photos (auto timestamped + GPS).

Mark dangerous areas in red → share with insurance & homeowner instantly.

Upload after photos + cleanup proof.

Auto-generate report → send to insurance company with cost breakdown.

2. Insurance Claim Disputes

If adjuster undervalues damage (like your $11,400 vs. $4,500 roof case), you have timestamped, geotagged photos to back your invoice.

Can send them annotated photos + a formal report instead of digging through phone galleries.

3. Subcontractor Oversight

If you sub out jobs (roofing, debris hauling, stump grinding), you can require photo check-ins.

Prevents disputes like “that wasn’t done” or “we didn’t break the fence.”

4. Crew Accountability

Know which crew was where, and when.

Protects you if a client says, “your guys didn’t show up” → you have timestamp proof.

5. Marketing / Before & After

Build instant galleries of “before and after” shots.

Use for social media, website, or Yelp/Google updates.

Shows professionalism without extra editing.

🚧 Things to Keep in Mind

Cost: Starts per-user monthly. Best value if you have multiple crews/projects running at once.

Training: Crews must remember to always open projects and snap pics inside the app (not their phone camera).

Connectivity: Works offline but syncs once you have service; good for rural or storm-hit areas.
