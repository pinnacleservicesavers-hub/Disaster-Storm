Here are the electric utilities, cooperatives (EMCs), and municipal/public power systems in Maine — with official links to their procurement, supplier/vendor registration pages, or where you can pursue contractor/vendor work (storm response, vegetation/ROW, emergency services, construction support, equipment/materials, etc.).

Maine’s electrical system consists of investor-owned utilities, electric cooperatives, and municipal utilities — many of which maintain procurement portals, vendor registration pages, or bid listings.

🌲 Electric Cooperatives (EMCs) in Maine

Maine has two main electric cooperatives serving rural areas:

3. Aroostook Electric Cooperative (AEC)

🔗 Official Website
https://www.aecoop.com

✔ Contact procurement/business office
✔ Many times they post RFPs and bid opportunities through their main site — check their “News” / “Business” pages

While AEC does not have an obvious online supplier portal, contacting their engineering or operations departments directly is recommended for contracting opportunities.

4. Washington Electric Cooperative (WEC)

🔗 Official Website
https://www.washingtonelectric.coop

✔ Contact their office for vendor/contractor registration
✔ They send out bid opportunities to pre-qualified vendors

WEC typically posts requests for proposals and procurement notices under News/Business or Member/Contractor links.
