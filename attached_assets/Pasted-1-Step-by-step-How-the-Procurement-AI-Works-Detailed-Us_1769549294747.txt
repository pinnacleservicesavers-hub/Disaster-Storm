1) Step-by-step: How the Procurement AI Works (Detailed User + System Flow)
Phase A — Contractor Onboarding & Data Capture

Contractor creates account

Role: Contractor (or Admin for company)

Verifies email + phone (OTP recommended)

Company profile setup

Legal business name (as registered)

DBA (if any)

Entity type (LLC / S-Corp / etc.)

Address + service areas

Primary contact & authorized signer

Payment method (for subscription)

Compliance & identifiers upload
The app requests and stores (securely):

EIN

UEI (SAM)

CAGE Code (if applicable)

DUNS (legacy only if a portal still asks)

NAICS codes + keywords describing services

PSC codes (optional but powerful)

State contractor license numbers (if required)

IFTA / DOT / MC numbers (if relevant)

Insurance: COI, GL, Auto, WC, Umbrella (as needed)

Bonding letter (if applicable)

W9 (either upload or auto-generate + e-sign)

Certifications (SDVOSB/WOSB/HUBZone/DBE/etc.)

Past performance references, resumes, equipment list

Procurement site selection
The contractor chooses which portals to activate:

SAM.gov / state portals / BidNet / utilities / municipalities

They set:

Region(s) to monitor

Minimum/maximum contract size

Bid types (services, construction, ROW, debris, etc.)

Set-aside categories (if certified)

Portal credentials + authorization

Contractor either:

(A) connects accounts via secure integration (OAuth if available), OR

(B) provides credentials in a secure vault (only if allowed), OR

(C) uses assisted/manual mode where AI prepares everything but contractor uploads/submits

Important: contractor confirms “AI can prepare bids and drafts, contractor approves final submission.”

Phase B — Opportunity Monitoring & Qualification

AI begins continuous monitoring

Scheduled scans (every X minutes/hour) per portal

Pulls new opportunities + updates existing ones

Tracks amendments and addenda

AI qualifies each opportunity
The AI checks:

NAICS/keyword match

Location match

Deadline feasibility

Required licensing / bonding / insurance

Disqualifiers (mandatory site visit, experience thresholds, etc.)

Risk score + complexity score

Opportunity is categorized

✅ “Auto-Eligible” (safe to proceed)

⚠️ “Needs Review” (missing requirement or uncertainty)

❌ “Not Eligible” (disqualifier detected)

Contractor gets notified

Email + SMS for important opportunities

App dashboard shows:

due date

pre-bid details

required attachments checklist

risk flags

Phase C — Bid Planning & Pre-bid Management

Pre-bid meeting/site visit detection

AI extracts:

date/time

location

meeting format

attendance requirement

AI auto-creates calendar event and reminders

AI prepares contractor’s pre-bid pack

Address + arrival instructions

PPE checklist

documents to bring

a “smart questions” list tailored to the scope

a draft RFI email template if needed

AI can send clarification questions

Contractor approves (or enables auto-send)

AI emails municipality/agency contact with precise questions

AI logs responses and attaches them to bid file

Phase D — Pricing & Strategy Engine

AI builds a pricing model

Pulls similar awarded bids (when available)

Uses internal data: contractor’s past bids + wins/losses

Uses regional cost multipliers (labor, fuel, mobilization)

Creates “recommended bid range” with:

conservative

target

aggressive (to win)

Contractor inputs their number

After site visit, contractor can type:

what they think it should be

notes about access, hazards, equipment needs

AI compares and explains
If contractor is too low:

identifies missing cost elements (labor hours, equipment, dump fees, traffic control, etc.)

warns about risk of losing money or nonperformance

If contractor is too high:

shows where the price deviates from historical award ranges

suggests optimization (scope language, alternates, value engineering)

Phase E — Proposal Assembly + Form Completion

AI assembles the full bid packet
Includes:

coversheet

executive summary

technical approach

staffing plan

equipment list

schedule

safety plan

compliance certifications

past performance

pricing sheet + line items

all mandatory forms

Auto-fill forms
The AI uses the contractor onboarding profile to fill:

W9

vendor forms

certifications

insurance details

NAICS/PSC

control numbers

UEI/CAGE/EIN

signature blocks (contractor provides authorized signer)

Version control
Every update creates:

a timestamped version

diff log of what changed

a “final submission copy” lock

Phase F — Approval + Submission

Approval request

AI sends SMS: “Bid ready for review”

AI emails the PDF pack + summary checklist

Contractor reviews in-app

Contractor chooses

Approve & Submit

Request edits

Hold / skip bid

Submission
Depending on portal rules:

Electronic submission: AI submits via integration or guided steps

Sealed bid: AI provides:

print instructions

mailing label

delivery checklist

calendar reminders

Submission confirmation

AI stores:

confirmation number

screenshot or receipt pdf

submission timestamp

copy of submitted files

Phase G — Post-Submission Tracking + Learning

Bid tracking

AI monitors:

amendments

award notices

bid tab releases

clarification requests

Contractor marks results

Won / lost / no award / cancelled

If won: AI triggers onboarding steps (COI, contract docs, kickoff)

If lost: AI runs loss analysis

Learning loop

AI compares:

award price vs your price

missing documents

evaluation factors

Adjusts future strategy recommendations

Improves win odds over time

2) AI Agent Workflow Logic (Developer Draft)
State machine

Each opportunity has a state:

DISCOVERED

QUALIFYING

NEEDS_REVIEW (missing info/disqualifier risk)

ELIGIBLE

PREBID_SCHEDULED

INFO_REQUESTED (RFI sent)

PRICING_MODEL_READY

PROPOSAL_DRAFTING

READY_FOR_REVIEW

CHANGES_REQUESTED

APPROVED_TO_SUBMIT

SUBMITTED

AWAITING_AWARD

WON / LOST / CANCELLED

Event-driven triggers

on_new_opportunity_found(opportunity)

create record → QUALIFYING

run eligibility scoring

if eligible → ELIGIBLE + notify

on_deadline_within_threshold(opportunity, hours)

increase urgency score

notify contractor with countdown

auto-prioritize in queue

on_prebid_detected(opportunity)

create calendar event

compose prep checklist

schedule reminders

on_docs_uploaded(company)

re-run eligibility for pending bids

update proposal templates

on_contractor_feedback_pricing(opportunity)

run variance analysis

update recommended bid range

update pricing narrative

on_contractor_approve(opportunity)

run compliance check

validate required forms present

submit OR provide sealed instructions

Guardrails

Before SUBMITTED:

Confirm:

all mandatory attachments exist

correct portal format naming rules

valid signatures

insurance meets minimums

If any fail:

block submission

notify contractor with exact fix list

Concurrency

The agent can draft many bids in parallel.

Only “submission” requires explicit approval.

A “Pending Approval Queue” holds multiple bids.

A) Not Legal Advice / Not Professional Advice

Disclaimer — Not Legal, Financial, or Procurement Advice
The Procurement AI tools in this application provide automated assistance, drafting, and guidance for convenience only. The information generated by the AI agent is not legal advice, financial advice, accounting advice, or a substitute for professional procurement consulting. You are solely responsible for reviewing all solicitations, requirements, forms, pricing, and submissions, and for consulting qualified professionals where necessary.

B) No Guarantee of Award

Disclaimer — No Guarantee of Winning Bids
Use of this application does not guarantee that any bid will be accepted or that any contract will be awarded. Procurement decisions are made by third parties (agencies, municipalities, utilities, and other buyers) based on evaluation criteria outside our control. While the platform may improve organization, responsiveness, and proposal quality—and may increase the likelihood of submitting compliant, competitive bids—results will vary and awards are never guaranteed.

C) Contractor Responsible for Final Submission

Disclaimer — Contractor Review & Approval Required
All bid packages, pricing recommendations, forms, and submission drafts generated by the AI agent must be reviewed and approved by the contractor before submission. The contractor is solely responsible for the accuracy, completeness, legality, and compliance of the bid, including all statements, certifications, pricing, and attachments.

D) Data Accuracy + Third-Party Reliance

Disclaimer — Third-Party Data & Portal Accuracy
The platform may use third-party data sources and procurement portals. We do not control and cannot guarantee the accuracy, availability, completeness, or timeliness of any third-party information, portal listings, deadlines, addenda, or award results. Users are responsible for independently verifying all critical details directly within the source solicitation and procurement portal.

E) Deadlines, Addenda, and Changes

Disclaimer — Deadlines and Addenda
Procurement solicitations may change at any time due to addenda, corrections, or portal updates. The contractor is responsible for monitoring deadlines and acknowledging addenda, even when the platform provides reminders. Failure to meet deadlines or respond to addenda may result in disqualification.

F) Pricing Disclaimer

Disclaimer — Pricing Recommendations
Any pricing ranges, bid recommendations, or “suggested bid amounts” are estimates generated using available information, historical patterns (when available), and contractor-provided inputs. These recommendations may be incomplete or inaccurate due to missing site conditions, scope changes, unknown risks, or unavailable award data. Contractors must validate all pricing and ensure profitability and feasibility.

G) Submission Method Disclaimer (Electronic vs Sealed)

Disclaimer — Submission Requirements
Some solicitations require sealed submissions, notarizations, physical delivery, bonds, or original signatures. The platform may provide instructions and reminders, but the contractor remains fully responsible for meeting the required submission method, format, and delivery requirements.

H) AI Limitations Disclaimer

Disclaimer — AI Output Limitations
AI-generated content may contain errors, omissions, or misinterpretations. The platform does not warrant that AI outputs will be accurate or suitable for any specific procurement process. Users must verify all content prior to use or submission.

I) Consent to Messaging

By enabling notifications, you consent to receive operational SMS, email, and in-app messages related to procurement opportunities, deadlines, and bid workflow. Message frequency may vary. Carrier fees may apply. You may opt out of non-essential messages at any time.
