This document defines the module order, phases, and high-level interactions for the Storm Ops app. Build the dashboard and navigation in this order and wire the modules to communicate as indicated below.

🚀 Recommended Workflow & Module Order
Phase 1: Awareness & Monitoring (before the storm hits)

Weather Center – Live weather radar, alerts, forecasts.

Storm Predictions – AI storm modeling and risk mapping.

Eyes in the Sky – Drone operations + aerial imaging.

Traffic Cam Watcher – Road conditions & evacuation routes.

Phase 2: Active Storm Operations (during/after impact)

Drone Operation – Real-time drone deployments with AI overlays.

AI Damage Detection – Automatic detection & measurement of damages (trees, roofs, flooding, etc.).

Disaster Lens – Contractor field photo/video capture with AI tools (measurements, highlights, water/gas line circles).

Claims Central – Auto-generate reports, measurements, adjuster packets.

Phase 3: Response & Coordination

Lead Management – Assign incoming jobs, track estimates.

Victim Portal – Homeowners/customers submit photos, see claim status, track crews.

Customer Hub – Customer communication, updates, billing.

Contractor Portal – Subcontractors manage work orders, submit compliance docs.

Contractor Command – HQ command dashboard for scheduling, payroll, crew coordination.

Phase 4: Support & Long-Term Recovery

Disaster Essentials Marketplace – Supplies, rentals, food, fuel, PPE for contractors & victims.

Storm Share – Community engagement: photos, updates, live maps, insurance/contractor collaboration.

🔗 Module Interactions

Weather Center & Storm Predictions feed into Eyes in the Sky and Traffic Cam Watcher.

Eyes in the Sky + Drone Operation push data into AI Damage Detection and Disaster Lens.

Disaster Lens feeds evidence into Claims Central and Victim Portal.

Lead Management assigns jobs pulled in from Victim Portal.

Contractor Portal & Contractor Command sync together (compliance, job dispatch, payments).

Customer Hub syncs with Claims Central (adjuster reports + homeowner updates).

Storm Share pulls highlights from Disaster Lens, Victim Portal, and Traffic Cams to share externally.

Marketplace connects to both Contractor Portal (supplies for crews) and Victim Portal (homeowner recovery supplies).

🧑‍🏫 Onboarding Explanation (simple for contractors)

When onboarding contractors, explain the workflow like this:

Monitor the storm (Modules 1–4).

Capture & detect damages (Modules 5–7).

Process claims & jobs (Modules 8–13).

Support recovery & community sharing (Modules 14–15).

Dev Notes (for Replit)

Build the dashboard with four phase sections in the order above.

Each module should be its own route/page (e.g., /weather, /predictions, /eyes, /traffic, …).

Implement a lightweight event bus (pub/sub) so modules can pass data without tight coupling (e.g., WEATHER.FORECAST, STORM.RISKMAP, DRONE.MEDIA.NEW, DAMAGE.FINDINGS, CLAIM.STATUS.UPDATE, JOB.NEW).

Keep labels exactly as written here for now.

No drag-and-drop needed at this stage—simple ordered layout is fine.
