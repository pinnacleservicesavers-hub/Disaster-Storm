What is GenAI?
GenAI is a shorthand for Generative Artificial Intelligence. It's a subset of AI technology focused on creating new content—like text, images, videos, music, or even code—based on patterns learned from vast amounts of training data. Unlike traditional AI (which analyzes or classifies existing data), GenAI generates original outputs that mimic human-like creativity.
Key Characteristics

Input-Driven Creation: You give it a prompt (e.g., "Write a poem about space"), and it produces something new.
Powered by Machine Learning: Uses models like transformers (the tech behind ChatGPT) trained on internet-scale data.
Probabilistic: Outputs aren't always perfect; they can be creative but sometimes "hallucinate" facts.

How It Works (Simplified)

Training: The model learns patterns from billions of examples (e.g., books, websites, images).
Generation: It predicts the "next token" (word/pixel) based on your input, building output step-by-step.
Refinement: Techniques like fine-tuning make it safer, more accurate, or specialized.
