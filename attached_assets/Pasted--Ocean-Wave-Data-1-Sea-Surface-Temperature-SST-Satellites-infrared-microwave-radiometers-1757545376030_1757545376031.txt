🌊 Ocean & Wave Data

1. Sea Surface Temperature (SST)

Satellites (infrared & microwave radiometers):
NOAA GOES, JPSS (Suomi-NPP, NOAA-20), NASA MODIS/VIIRS provide global SST.

Buoys & Ships: NOAA’s National Data Buoy Center (NDBC) measures water temps at fixed locations.

Argo Floats: A network of 3,000+ drifting floats that measure ocean temp/salinity profiles.

2. Wave Height, Period, and Direction

NDBC Buoys: Direct wave measurements (significant height, period, direction).

Satellite Altimeters: (e.g., Jason-3, Sentinel-6) use radar altimetry to estimate wave height and sea level.

Numerical Models:

NOAA WAVEWATCH III (WW3): a global wave model driven by wind forecasts.

ECMWF WAM model: European equivalent.

3. Wave Strength/Impact

Derived from significant wave height + wave period + direction, often combined with local bathymetry (coastal ocean models).

🌀 Hurricane Strength & Structure

1. Central Pressure & Winds (Intensity)

Aircraft Reconnaissance ("Hurricane Hunters")

Dropsondes measure pressure, temp, humidity, winds inside the storm.

Stepped-frequency microwave radiometers (SFMR) measure surface winds over ocean.

Satellites:

Scatterometers (ASCAT, OSCAT) measure surface wind speeds/direction.

Infrared imagery shows cloud top temps (proxy for convection strength).

Microwave sounders (e.g., AMSU, ATMS) reveal internal storm structure.

GOES geostationary satellites provide rapid scans of storm eye, convection bursts, and cloud motion vectors.

2. Storm Surge & Wave Coupling

Coupled ocean-atmosphere models (like HWRF + MOM6, ADCIRC, SLOSH) simulate surge heights, currents, and wave interaction.

3. Track & Intensity Forecasts

Models:

HWRF, HAFS (NOAA’s new Hurricane Analysis and Forecast System), HMON — high-res hurricane-specific models.

GFS, ECMWF — global models that feed boundary conditions.

Ensembles: show probability of tracks/intensity.

4. Official Products (NHC)

Tropical Cyclone Public Advisories (track, winds, pressure, hazards).

Forecast cones, wind speed probability graphics, surge/wave outlooks.

Data ingested from all the above sources.

⚡ Summary Table
Parameter	Data Sources
Ocean temp (SST)	Satellites (GOES, VIIRS, MODIS), buoys, Argo floats
Wave height/strength	NDBC buoys, satellite altimetry, WAVEWATCH III model
Hurricane winds/pressure	Hurricane Hunter aircraft, scatterometer satellites, dropsondes, SFMR
Hurricane structure	Microwave/infrared satellite imagery, radar (land-based & airborne)
Hurricane forecasts	Models (HWRF, HAFS, HMON, GFS, ECMWF), NHC advisories

✅ So: ocean temps/waves = satellites + buoys + wave models.
✅ Hurricane strength = aircraft recon + satellites + models, compiled and published by the National Hurricane Center (NHC).
