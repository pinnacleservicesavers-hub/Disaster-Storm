POSSIBLE CAUSES (A → E)
A) Faulty Transmission Downshift / Torque Converter Lockup

Description:
The transmission should modulate shifts smoothly, especially downhill with engine braking. If the torque converter is locking and unlocking erratically or the TCM is commanding harsh shifts, you get a sudden jerk.

Likely Sensors Involved:

Transmission speed sensors

Gear position sensors

TCM (Transmission Control Module)

Ticking Noise:
Can sometimes happen if the converter stutters or engages incorrectly.

Severity:
🚨 High — could damage transmission if driven hard

Estimate in Columbus / Cataula, GA:

Diagnostic scan: $100–$150

Torque converter replacement: $1,800 – $3,000+

Rebuild transmission: $2,800 – $4,500+

B) Transmission Control Module (TCM) / Software Fault

Description:
Modern Broncos rely heavily on computer control for shifting. If the TCM is glitching, it can command shifts incorrectly based on sensor data.

Ticking Noise:
Possible electrical relay tick or solenoid activation.

Severity:
⚠ Moderate; usually software or control module

Estimate:

Dealer reflash / software update: Free – $150

TCM replacement: $600 – $1,200

Wiring check: $100 – $300

Often this can be resolved by an ECU/TCM update at the dealer under warranty.

C) Valve Body / Transmission Solenoid Pack Issue

Description:
Inside the transmission is a valve body that controls fluid flow to change gears. A bad solenoid or valve can cause jerky shifts.

Ticking Noise:
Hydraulic solenoids often make a tick-like sound.

Severity:
⚠ Significant — performance issue

Estimate:

Solenoid pack replacement: $500 – $1,000

Valve body replacement: $900 – $1,800

D) Engine Mechanical / Valvetrain Tick

Description:
Ticking that’s internal to the engine (lifter, cam, valve) may not directly cause jerking, but it’s a real engine issue that must be ruled out — especially if oil pressure is involved.

Severity:
⚠ Moderate to severe depending on cause

Estimate:

Lifters: $300 – $800

Camshaft / internal repair: $1,000 – $3,000+

Timing chain: $600 – $1,500

E) Transfer Case / AWD System Binding

Description:
If in 4WD (4H or 4A) and the transfer case is binding, it can result in abrupt jerks downhill.

Severity:
⚠ Moderate

Estimate:

Transfer case service: $200 – $500

Transfer case sensor or actuator: $250 – $700

F) Brake System Control / ABS Intervention During Descent

Description:
ABS or traction control can activate if it misinterprets wheel speed going downhill. It might feel like a “jerk” or grab.

Severity:
⚠ Low to moderate

Estimate:

ABS sensor replacement: $150 – $300

Wheel speed sensor cleaning/repair: $50 – $150
