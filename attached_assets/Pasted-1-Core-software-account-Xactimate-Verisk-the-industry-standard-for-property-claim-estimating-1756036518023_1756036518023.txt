2) Hardware / system prerequisites

Windows 10/11 PC for Xactimate Desktop X1 (fast CPU, plenty of RAM/SSD), or use Chrome/Edge for Xactimate Online. Stable broadband required. Mac users typically run the Online version in a browser.

3) Pricing data (critical)

Pull the local Verisk price list for each claim (by ZIP/city) inside X1 (Claim Info → Parameters → Request Price List). Update regularly so your line-item rates match what carriers expect.

4) Training & proof of skill

Do Verisk’s training and consider Xactimate User Certification Levels 1–3 (valid 2 years). Level 1 = fundamentals; Level 2/3 = advanced proficiency. This credential helps reduce pushback on your estimates.

5) Field documentation kit (for tree & storm losses)

Photos & video: “before / during / after,” wide → mid → close, plus compass directions.

Measurements: tape/laser (DBH for trees, length/height, reach/rigging distances), job sketches.

6) Tree-work estimating approach (what adjusters expect to see)

Break out Emergency services (after-hours mobilization, hazard mitigation, traffic control), Access/rigging & crane time (by hour/ton-meter class if applicable), Tree removal (per tree, include DBH/complexity), Debris handling (haul-off/chipping/fees), Protection (tarping, fencing), and Follow-up (stump grind, backfill/grade, repair items).

Use macros/templates in Xactimate so every tree-on-structure job is consistent (you can save a “Tree on Dwelling – Crane” macro with your standard line items & notes).

7) Carrier-facing notes

In your Line Item Notes, include: tree species (if known), DBH, fall direction, proximity to energized lines, structure impacted, access constraints, equipment required (crane size, mats), crew size & hours, and why each task was reasonable & necessary (R&N) for safety and mitigation.

8) Alternatives (some carriers use these)
