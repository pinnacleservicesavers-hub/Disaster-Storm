The “AI Weather Brain” you’ll install
1) Real-time fusion & nowcasting

Ingest NEXRAD Level-II (AWS S3) every scan; derive storm motion vectors from sequential velocity/reflectivity fields. 
registry.opendata.aws

Overlay MRMS AzShear/Rotation Tracks to identify persistent mesocyclone cores; extrapolate 10–30-min swath polygons (centerline + uncertainty corridor).

registry.opendata.aws

Use HRRR to bias near-future motion (storm-relative winds) and rainfall rates; use SPC watches to raise confidence thresholds. 
Rapid Refresh
+1

2) Street-level impact scoring (contractor-ready)

For each 250–500 m grid (snap to streets later):

Wind/Gust risk (HRRR at 10 m + downburst proxies)

Tornado proximity (distance to AzShear track centerline, recency)

Hail proxy (dual-pol + MRMS hail size if you add it)

Rain rate / flash-flood proxy (radar QPE + HRRR)

Tree-fall potential = f(wind gusts, soil saturation, canopy/land cover)

Score 0–100 and classify: Green / Yellow / Red (Deploy / Hold / Avoid)

3) “Contractor Guidance Engine”

Rules + ML:

Stage Here: hotels/fuels within a 30-mile ring upwind of likely impact in next 12–24 h.

Go/No-Go windows: avoid Red cells (tornado core) but position on the right-front quadrant of post-track for tree/wind work.

Task predictions: likely jobs (trees on structures, powerlines, roads) by census block based on wind + canopy + prior claims density (if you collect).

Auto-briefs: per-county one-pagers: “ETA squall line 2:10–2:40 pm; gusts 55–65 mph; tornado potential elevated along Hwy-xx; best staging: Exit 12 hotels.”

4) Frontend map layers (what users see)

Base: vector tiles (streets + parcels).

Radar: reflectivity/velocity tiles at 256 px (WebGL).

Rotation Swath: animated polyline with 10–30-min extrapolation cone.

NWS alerts: polygons with color + timer.

Hurricane cone & wind radii: from NHC GeoJSON.

Risk heatmap: the 0–100 score (tap = explanation + checklist).

Live cams/chaser feeds: panels you can embed/curate.

Implementation stack (concrete)

Ingest/Decode

Read NEXRAD Level II from s3://noaa-nexrad-level2/... (AWS Open Data). Decode with PyART/MetPy → grid → Cloud-optimized GeoTIFFs or MVT tiles. 
registry.opendata.aws

Pull MRMS AzShear/Rotation Tracks (NetCDF/GRIB). Convert to vector tracks for map. 
training.weather.gov

Subscribe to NWS Alerts API (api.weather.gov/alerts?area=GA,AL,...). 
National Weather Service

Fetch HRRR from NOMADS (GRIB2), downscale to your grid, cache last 6 runs. 
nomads.ncep.noaa.gov

Load NHC GIS advisories each cycle (shapefiles/GeoJSON) for tropical. 
National Hurricane Center

Processing

Kafka (events) → workers (Python) → PostGIS/Redis caches.

Build storm vectors from radar motion (Lucas-Kanade / optical flow) and fit linear motion for 10–30 min.

Compute the risk score per grid; publish as vector tiles (Mapbox/Tippecanoe).

WebSocket pushes for alerts to logged-in contractors.

UI/UX

Mapbox GL or Leaflet + WebGL overlays.

“Deploy Plan” panel: hotel/fuel lists, distances, live availability (your DEM marketplace).

“Likely Calls” list for each contractor territory.

Add live video the right way

Chaser feeds (license/partnerships): SevereStudios, Live Storms Media, etc. Embed YouTube Live/Twitch players via their APIs.

511 traffic cams per state (free/public; varies by DOT). Great for wind damage confirmation.

Your uploader: Spotter/contractor uploads (HLS from phone → your S3).
(These sources complement, not replace, the official data.)
