Here are the Electric Member Cooperatives (EMCs) in Colorado — these are the member-owned rural utilities/distribution co-ops you can register with as a contractor/vendor for services like storm response, vegetation/right-of-way (ROW) clearing, debris removal, emergency restoration, vegetation management, hauling, equipment support, and utility-related contracting.

(Colorado doesn’t use “EMC” as commonly as some states — they’re normally called “electric cooperatives” or “electric associations.” But they serve the same purpose.)

📌 Why You Should Register with Colorado EMCs

Even though Colorado is more heavily served by investor-owned utilities (like Xcel and Black Hills), these electric cooperatives are very active locally and can be great sources of contractor work — especially for:

✔ Vegetation/right-of-way clearing
✔ Emergency response after storms or fires
✔ Equipment support and hauling
✔ Local utility contracting
✔ Vegetation management programs
✔ Tree removal near lines
✔ Subcontracting for restoration projects

Unlike IOUs, cooperatives are member-focused utilities — but they often contract out work to qualified vendors/contractors because they don’t have large internal crews.
