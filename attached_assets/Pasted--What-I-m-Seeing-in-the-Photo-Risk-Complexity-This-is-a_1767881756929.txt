✅ What It Would Take to Remove This Tree (Step-by-Step)

Here’s the exact process a professional tree crew would follow:

1) Site Inspection + Safety Plan

They would:

check the lean direction

identify line voltage risk (service line vs primary lines)

assess limb weight distribution

check driveway and equipment access

plan cut sequence

📌 This is critical because the wrong first cut could swing limbs into the lines or house.

2) Utility Risk Evaluation

If the lines in your picture are:

service drop lines (to the house) → can often work around with caution

primary distribution lines → most companies WILL NOT work near it without utility involvement

✅ The tree is intertwined with lines, meaning:

more time

more careful cuts

more rigging

3) Setup Work

Crew setup includes:

cones + safety perimeter

ground crew clearing workspace

rigging ropes + pulleys installed

possibly a bucket truck or climber

4) Remove Tree in Sections

The crew would:

remove outer limbs first

cut and lower branches away from the lines

rig each major limb so it doesn’t swing

dismantle the trunk top-down

cut trunk into sections and lower

✅ THIS is why the job is expensive:
It’s slow, controlled cutting, not simply dropping the tree.

5) Haul Away Brush and Wood

Includes:

chipping brush

hauling logs

raking yard

leaf blower final cleanup

(Some companies charge extra for hauling logs, but most include it.)

6) Stump Grinding

stump is ground down usually 6–12 inches below grade

grindings are either:
✅ left in place
✅ hauled away (extra charge)
