Here are official FEMA (and FEMA-aligned) links with templates, forms, checklists, and Excel tools you can use to build FEMA-ready timesheets, equipment logs, load tickets, and other documentation for compliance and auditing:

📄 1. FEMA Public Assistance Project Templates & Forms (Direct from FEMA)

👉 Public Assistance Project Templates and Forms – search/download many PA forms (including Force Account Labor Summary, Force Account Equipment Summary, and other standardized templates you can adapt for FEMA compliance) 📥
https://www.fema.gov/assistance/public/tools-resources/templates-forms

⚠️ Note: not all are Excel, but most are downloadable and can be adapted to Excel or Google Sheets.

📌 Examples of forms listed here:

Force Account Equipment Summary – FEMA Form FF-104-FY-21-141

Force Account Labor Summary – FEMA Form FF-104-FY-21-137
👉 These can be turned into timesheet and equipment tracking spreadsheets.

📋 2. Public Assistance Forms & Job Aids (Guides + Templates)

👉 Public Assistance Forms & Training page – includes forms and job aids for documenting labor, equipment, load tickets, fringe benefits, and more in FEMA Public Assistance programs:
https://www.vaemergency.gov/divisions/finance/grants/recovery-grant-programs/public-assistance-resources

On this page you’ll find downloadable tools like:

Activity Report (tracks hours & work performed)

Equipment & Material Log

FEMA Documentation Guide

Fringe Benefit Form

Labor-Backfill Roster

Load Ticket (with instructions and job aid)

Monitor Log

Truck Certification Instructions
👉 These are typically Word/PDF but easily converted to Excel.

📊 3. State / Template Examples (Excel Tools You Can Use)

👉 FEMA-style disaster log & equipment templates (Excel) – example Excel worksheets used for equipment logs that follow the FEMA documentation style:
https://www.tnwarn.org/downloads/FEMADisasterLogstemplates.xls

These aren’t official FEMA forms, but they mirror how FEMA wants equipment and labor logged — and they can be dropped directly into your compliance binder.

📑 4. Additional FEMA & Compliance Resources

👉 FEMA NIMS ICS Forms Booklet (PDF) – includes standardized Incident Command forms that help with documentation, time tracking, incident logs, and resource check-in/out that supports FEMA compliance.
Accessible as a PDF booklet with key incident and resource forms.

🔧 Tips for Using These Templates

✔ Convert to Excel or Google Sheets – Most FEMA forms are PDF/Word. You can copy fields into spreadsheets to manage multiple crews and days.
✔ Match FEMA Terms – Use the exact field names FEMA uses (e.g., “Incident #”, “Work Location”, “Employee Classification”) to reduce audit questions.
✔ Tie to Project Worksheet (PW) – All logs should reference the FEMA PW # and Incident #.
✔ Signatures & Dates – Include spaces for supervisor and employee signatures and dates.
