SBA Disaster Assistance Guide

When disaster strikes—whether floods, wildfires, or hurricanes—the U.S. Small Business Administration (SBA) provides low-interest disaster loans to help individuals, families, businesses, and nonprofits recover. These loans are designed to cover losses that insurance and other aid do not fully address.

Who Can Apply for SBA Disaster Loans

SBA disaster loans are available to a wide range of people and organizations, as long as they are in a declared disaster area and meet eligibility requirements:

Businesses of all sizes

Homeowners

Renters

Private nonprofit organizations

Common Disasters Covered

SBA provides disaster assistance for a variety of federally declared events, including:

Texas Floods – Assistance for homes, businesses, and renters affected by severe flooding.

California Wildfires – Support for losses caused by fire and smoke damage.

Hurricane Milton – Recovery loans for wind and storm damage.

Hurricane Helene – Financial aid for those impacted by large-scale hurricane destruction.

Steps to Apply

Check Disaster Declarations
Confirm whether your area has been declared a disaster zone by the SBA.

Apply for a Disaster Loan
Submit your application if your business, home, or rental property has been affected.

Check Your Status
Use the MySBA Loan Portal or check your email for updates on your application progress.

What SBA Disaster Loans Can Be Used For

Uninsured Losses: Cover costs not reimbursed by insurance or FEMA grants.

Business Operating Expenses: Pay bills, payroll, or costs that could have been covered had the disaster not occurred.

Types of SBA Disaster Loans
1. Physical Damage Loans

Covers repairs or replacement of physical assets (homes, businesses, equipment, inventory).

Includes Mitigation Assistance: Additional funding to make improvements that prevent or reduce future damage.

2. Economic Injury Disaster Loans (EIDL)

Helps small businesses cover essential operating expenses after a declared disaster.

Can be used to pay bills, rent, utilities, and other working capital needs.

3. Military Reservist Loan

Provides financial help to small businesses facing hardship when a key employee is called to active duty.

Covers necessary operating costs during the employee’s absence.
