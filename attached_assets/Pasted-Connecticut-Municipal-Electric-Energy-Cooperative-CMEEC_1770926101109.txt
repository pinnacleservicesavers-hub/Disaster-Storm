Connecticut Municipal Electric Energy Cooperative (CMEEC)

This is the main cooperative entity for municipal utilities in Connecticut — it supplies wholesale power to several local utilities that serve customers across the state.

📌 How This Works (Co-op Structure)

• CMEEC is not a direct EMC (like Georgia EMCs), but a joint-action cooperative formed by municipal utilities to pool resources and wholesale power supply.
• Each municipal utility is an owner/participant in CMEEC and is its own local utility — often contract opportunities come through the individual utility rather than directly through CMEEC.

🧠 Procurement & Working With Them

If you want to work with utilities in Connecticut:

🔹 Start with CMEEC — request supplier/vendor information, bidding opportunities, and cooperative projects.
🔹 Also contact each municipal utility — they often have local procurement portals, RFPs, or supplier registration pages.
🔹 Regulatory info can be found through the Connecticut Public Utilities Regulatory Authority (PURA) for supplier compliance and licensing. (Ask me if you want links for suppliers/vendors with PURA.)
